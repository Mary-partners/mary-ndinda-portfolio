What I'm Building

I don't just think about problems. I build systems that solve them. Here's what's on the workbench and what's already in the world.

The Building Philosophy

There's a difference between having ideas and building infrastructure. Ideas are everywhere. What's rare is the ability to take an insight, pressure-test it against operational reality, and turn it into something that works reliably across different contexts, teams, and pressures.

Everything I build follows a common logic: take a pattern I've seen across multiple disciplines, codify it into a repeatable system, and deploy it where the need is highest. I don't build for elegance. I build for endurance.

Anticipatory Liquidity Facility Pre-positioned capital mechanisms for agricultural lenders, structured to deploy liquidity before climate shocks become credit defaults. Designed for Fortune Credit's 10,000+ borrower portfolio. Live

What I've Already Built

The projects above aren't isolated experiments. They're built on a foundation of work that spans multiple organisations, geographies, and disciplines:

  • Cross-border payment infrastructure Central bank licensing and operational scaling for remittance corridors between Africa and Asia, scaling to $100M in volumes
  • Enterprise acceleration systems — Structured pathways serving 35,000+ borrowers (65% women) that converted catalytic capital into sustainable businesses
  • Multi-country programme architecture — Innovation portfolio management across 7+ countries within programmes valued at up to USD 70M
  • National disaster risk financing strategy — Contributed to Kenya's 2026–2030 framework with the National Treasury and IIED
  • Investment readiness programmes — Coached 1,000+ entrepreneurs through structured venture-building pathways across three organisations
  • Gender-intentional financial systems — Designed agricultural finance products that deliberately centre women borrowers, including the AFIYA programme spanning three countries

The common thread: Every system I build is designed to work without me. If it requires my presence to function, it's not a system — it's a dependency. I build for institutional endurance, not personal indispensability.
